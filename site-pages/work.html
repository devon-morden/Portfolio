Passion Project

With my background in Classics, Linguistics, and Education, it is no wonder that I am passionate about teaching Latin. And I think that even beginners can read Latin without translating it into English first. To that end, I have begun to develop what, to the exten of my knowledge, I believe is a novel approach to learning and reading the language. Working through backwards design, I asked myself the following:

What should a learner be able to do when they have "learned" Latin?
Read Latin fluently, left-to-right.

What does an expert do to read fluently, left-to-right?
They use an internal decision tree to make predictions and either affirm or reject them as they proceed through the sentence.

How do we make predictions when reading?
We encounter familiar patterns.

But Latin doesn't have sentence patterns!
Latin absolutely has sentence patterns, it just shifts syntax to emphasize or de-emphasize sentence elements.

Follow the link in the title to see the work-in-progress! (And perhaps you'll learn a bit of Latin too)

K-12 DEI Curriculum

Special project grant to build a team and develop a K-12 Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Curriculum

Summary

After successfully petitioning school administration, I was granted funding to lead a small team to develop a K-12 DEI Curriculum. We held work sessions over the summer to develop clear problem statements & to begin outlining the curriculum. Work continued throughout the years as we brought the curriculum to various stakeholders for revisions and sign-off. The curriculum included not only the content & lessons to be delivered to students, but also the faculty training and school community events throughout the year. To my knowledge, this curriculum is still being widely implemented to this day.
