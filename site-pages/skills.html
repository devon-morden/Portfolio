Five Whys
As a former teacher and certified instructional designer, it has been my job to ask questions. Whether students or executives, one of the most meaningful things we can say is 'Please explain that to me.'
Data
While asking 'why' can be an excellent way to get the conversation heading the right direction, it is also important to keep ourselves objective by checking our assumptions against data when it is available. I was a data-driven teacher, and my experience in marketing has only solidified my data analysis skills.

UX Design

Active Listening
Understanding a user's experience hinges critically on one's ability to listen actively. I not only completed training in active listening as part of my role as a student advisor, I also completed Jen Cort's Challenging Conversations training as I took on the role of Middle School Diversity Coordinator. That was a role that required strong active listening skills as I worked with students and parents from a wide variety of backgrounds and beliefs to create an age-appropriate environment and educational experience that afforded safe classroom and extracurricular interactions during a period of intensely polarized politics social conflict.
Formal Training
As part of my coursework in learning experience design, I practiced UX research, UX design, and UI design. All of these skills are crucial to the development of quality learning experiences.
